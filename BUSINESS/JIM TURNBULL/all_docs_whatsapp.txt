TRANSYLVANIA FOOD COMPANY SRL
Group Structure 
The Food Development Company Ltd (FDC) is registered in UK and its 100% owned subsidiary, Transylvania Food Company Srl (TFC) is registered in Romania. FDC, the holding company in UK, is owned by two companies, Belmont Management Consultants Ltd owned by director and CEO Jim Turnbull and his wife, Salama Ltd owned by director and CFO Sir Murray Pringle and his two children, plus 29 other private shareholders, including TFC staff members. 
In 2010, Jim Turnbull was appointed, and remains, the sole representative of the FDC shareholders in Romania.
Property 
Registered property 354 Saschiz (1,902 m²) purchased in 2010 and renovated to provide accommodation, offices, retail shop, process hall, cold storage, distillery, dry goods storage. 
Equipment to process cordials, preserves, bottle honey and to distil alcohol.
Ten-year lease from 2021 on plot (340 m²) in neighbour’s field where we have installed solar panels.
Intellectual Assets
Transylvania Food Company Srl registered and operational in Romania since 2010
Management team in place
Registered domains: PivnitaBunicii.com, PivnitaBunicii.ro, FoodDevCo.com
Registered brands: Pivnita Bunicii, Kaspers, Taste Transylvania
High level of Pivnita Bunicii brand recognition in Romania, growing recognition of Kaspers
Strong social / business impact 
Range of quality products with proven recipes already on the market
Staff trained to produce these products.
Elderflower concentrate production know-how established since 2010.
Network of collectors for wild elderflower
Recipe for elderflower gin developed by Herriot Watt University, Dept of Brewing & Distilling
Spirits production know-how established.
Licences for micro distillery, warehousing duty suspended alcohol, selling alcohol and serving alcohol.
Gin awarded two stars in 2022 by the International Taste Institute in Brussels. 
Elderflower Schnapps awarded two stars and Rhubarb Liqueur one star in 2024.
BRC Food Safety certified, with associated intellectual property.
All Pivnita Bunicii products now carbon neutral, a claim that can be made on the labels.
ESG self-audited but with documents to justify, scoring 80% and categorised as leading on compliance. 
Bio certification to process blueberry jam for private client.
Online shop – see www.PivnitaBunicii.com.
Back-office systems are cloud based: purchasing, inventory, manufacturing with trade and on-line sales synchronising for financial management. Retail sales from the shop use separate software.
Environmental Impact
Carbon footprint certification with offset in Kenya, a small step but one that hopefully others will follow.
Regenerative agriculture – planting trees in Saschiz, and others are now following.
Process waste composted.
General waste and packaging waste offset. 
Kaspers registered for bottle return scheme.
Single use plastic removed from our packaging and replaced with recycled paper.
Solar panels, generating 32% of our electricity consumed in 2022 and saving 14,856 g of CO₂ emissions.
Replaced all light bulbs and neon tubes with LED.
Two EV charging pillars, each with two charging points.
Leading by example to get litter picking undertaken in Saschiz.
Social Impact 
Certified Social Enterprise, with profits reinvested to build the business. 
We buy our raw materials (flowers, fruit, vegetables & honey) from the community.
We employ 14 local people from the community to produce our jams, chutney, cordials and spirits.
Network of approximately 1,500 people, mostly disadvantaged Roma, picking 112 tonne of elderflower in 2023, 54% of our total social impact.
This network is also used to collect wild plants, fruit and berries.
To date we have purchased 1,000 tonnes of raw materials with a cumulative value of Lei 3.9 million.
Putting money into the grass roots community creates a “bubble up” effect that stimulates the local economy and creates significant social impact.
Governance 
Accountability in our financial control policies
Transparency of our systems when dealing with the authorities
Employment contracts in place for all staff and seasonal workers
Our lowest paid workers receive the national living wage, which is above the minimum wage.
Contracts Sales
Honey supplied under the Highgrove label for sale at the three Highgrove shops, at Fortnum & Mason (London, Tokyo, and New York) plus on-line from Fortnum & Mason and Highgrove websites. We continue to have a strong relationship with King Charles and were invited to meet him when he visited Romania in June.
Contracts with supermarkets: Mega Image (Belgian owned supermarket chain with 585 stores in Romania), Auchan (French owned hypermarket chain with 33 stores in Romania) and eMAG a dominant online platform.
Production of blueberry jam and blueberry schnapps for Merry Berry farming group.
Relationship with SHS Drinks 
Our initial business plan was to establish a social enterprise and the first round of investment in 2010 was based on a letter of intent from Bottlegreen Drinks (BG) backed up by a rolling supply contract for elderflower concentrate, pre-financed on terms to maintain a positive cashflow. We were to supply 19,000 litres in 2010 rising to at least 8 tankers (160,000 litres) per year by 2016 and on this basis, we built our process facility in 2011/12. Initially we were treated as part of the family then Bottlegreen was bought by the SHS Drinks Division in 2012 but we had little contact with them until 2016 when we became a numbered supplier. We soon recognised the risk of our dependence on one big unfriendly customer and started to diversify our product portfolio.
We were hit badly in 2020/21 with reduced demand from SHS due to covid and then in 2022 rapidly escalating costs and difficulties as a result of the war in Ukraine. 
After years of lower-than-expected volumes and lower than anticipated prices from SHS we were finally making progress in 2023, with 160,000 litres supplied to SHS, when they announced that in response to their lower sales, they were restructuring their supply chain and that we would not be part of it. 
Exciting opportunities for 2026 and beyond
In late 2023 we contacted another major producer of elderflower non-alcoholic drinks in the UK. After several rounds of discussions, we were considered too expensive. In June 2025, we contacted them again and this time managed to establish that the previous price comparison had not compared like with like. Their technical team visited Saschiz in November and will result in a letter of intent for them to purchase 16,000 litres in 2026, 100,000 litres in 2027 and much more beyond.
In 2024, we were approached by a company interested to buy chilled fresh elderflowers for freezing at their facility in Hungary. The logistics are complicated, but a price was agreed for 80,000kg, but nothing happened in 2024 or 25. Then they contacted us again in July and we have agreed a price for at least 40,000kg in 2026, with considerable growth in future demand.
Our ideal investor is strategic, bringing sales and marketing expertise. This investment opportunity is in TFC and offers a balanced approach combining bank finance, grants, and investor capital, with a clear timeline to profitability and a strong social impact through rural job preservation. We invite you to join us in this promising venture to drive growth and create lasting value.

=== Letter to Cristi and Tudor 140126.docx ===
14/01/26
Dear Tudor
Established in 2010, we produce a range of sweet and savoury preserves (chutney), cordials (syrups), and we have a micro distillery producing gin, schnapps and liqueurs. These products are all based on the innovative use of local fruit and vegetables. We also bottle local honey and are certainly interested to export to Singapore. 
We are based in Saschiz, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, 20km south of Sighişoara.
We produce elderflower cordial, and this has involved 1,500 people wild harvesting the flowers.
We introduced savoury preserves to Romania and now sell more than our jams. 
We developed the recipes for our elderflower gin, elderflower schnapps and rhubarb liqueur and all have been recognised with stars by the International Taste Institute in Brussels
We export honey to Fortnum & Mason in London under the brand of King Charles III.
We are certified to a global standard for food safety, the first SME to achieve this in Romania. 
We were the first micro enterprise to obtain a distilling licence in our county, if not the country.
We will be happy for you to visit us in Saschiz. Please let us know of your interest and availability to meet.
Yours sincerely
Jim Turnbull

=== Thoughts 250126.docx ===
It has been suggested that we establish a work point in Bucharest (a physical space where actual activities take place). I have discussed with colleagues in the UK and we do not agree with it being considered the headquarters for TFC unless Doru buys the company, but it could be work point where we hold stock, if that would count as activities. 
We understand that Carrefour have a programme to sell local products and today we delivered our fist products to Carrefour in Tirgu Mures – under the banner produced in Mures County.
Doru could sell our stock to Carrefour, but I do not think we can qualify as producer local to Bucharest, as by law must declare place of production on the label and this must be registered and approved by ANSVA, so placing a “machine or two” in the Bucharest work point would not qualify. We are inspected several times per year by DSVA. I do not understand why we would supply without labels. Trading standards in Romania are very strict and the labels must be correct and facilitate full traceability. We can deliver from Saschiz for national distribution by Carrefour. 
Doru has fresh product rejected and this could indeed be used to make pickles, but are we talking about vegetables in vinegar or brine? Is there a market he can access? Does he have the recipes? Or are we talking about our range of savoury preserves?
We are certainly interested to establish what vegetables he throws out and if they could be used in our savoury products, changing the cost of disposal to a small amount of revenue for Doru.
It was suggested that Doru buys 350 k of product from TFC – Lei or Euro, but still a huge amount of product for us to produce and Doru to sell.
We have three product categories: in glass containers (jam, chutney, cordial, alcohol); elderflower concentrate; and chilled elderflowers. We are making progress to scale up our products in glass, but this alone is not enough to make TFC sustainable. We need all three categories, but our alcohol and bulk elderflower products are key.
In 2022 our UK customer was complementing us on the quality we produced and pushing us to expand and then without warning in 2023 cancelled our contract. We have sought to replace this one large customer with at least two smaller ones and are very close to signing a contract with a British company to supply 15,000 litres in 2026, worth Lei 184,000 in sales with a Lei 78,000 contribution (42%), rising sevenfold and more in 2027. The process is quite different to the 160,000 litres we produced in 2023 and we need to first demonstrate that we can produce the required quality (as we had to do for our previous customer in 2010). They are already talking about expanding our capacity to produce much bigger quantities.
We are also close to signing a second elderflower contract with the Hungarian subsidiary of a German company. Again, the process is very different, and we need to prove we can deliver. The initial quantity for 2026 is worth Lei750,000 with a Lei 250,000 contribution and with potential to double in 2027. They are interested in the long term and want to increase to much larger quantities.
Both of the elderflower contracts are for export and require that we are certified to an international food safety standard. We have been certified since 2016 but to maintain this we need to train our staff and to retain them over the winter – which is why we make products in glass. Our salaries and contribution to Buget de Stat runs at Lei 60,000 per month, even after reducing our staff by one third.
We are urgently seeking investment that will enable us to embark on this journey, with huge opportunities to scale up.

## Detailed Breakdown

### British Columbia (EGBC) - RECOMMENDED

**Why Best for Tudor:**
- **5+ years experience = potential exam waiver** - Tudor has 13+ years
- **No mandatory Canadian experience** before applying
- **Direct P.Eng application** (skip EIT stage)
- **CTI France MRA recognized** - sets precedent for EU degrees
- **Most flexible** with international credentials

**Process:**
1. WES ICAP Course-by-Course evaluation
2. Apply directly for P.Eng (not EIT)
3. Experience review - may waive/reduce academic exams
4. NPPE (law & ethics exam)
5. BC Online Seminar

**Key Quote:** "If you have 5+ years professional engineering experience, EGBC suggests applying directly for P.Eng for potential reduction or waiver of academic examinations."

**Cost:** ~$472 CAD application fee
**Timeline:** 4-6 months

**Links:**
- https://www.egbc.ca/how-to-apply/register-as-an-engineer/professional-engineer
- https://www.egbc.ca/Registration/Individual-Registrants/How-to-Apply/Trainees/EIT-First-Time-Applying-in-Canada

---

### Alberta (APEGA) - GOOD OPTION

**Advantages:**
- 50% of applicants are international (experienced process)
- **Licensee category** for non-residents/non-PR
- Can apply **before arriving in Canada**
- WES ICAP accepted

**Process:**
1. WES ICAP evaluation
2. Apply as Licensee (no citizenship/PR needed)
3. 48 months total experience (can be international)
4. NPPE exam
5. Possible technical exams

**Note:** "Licensee or Professional Member have same requirements, only difference is Licensee doesn't need citizenship/PR."

**Cost:** ~$500 CAD
**Timeline:** 3-6 months

**Links:**
- https://www.apega.ca/apply/membership/newcomers
- https://www.apega.ca/about-apega/publications/yourpeg/internationally-trained-professionals

---

### Ontario (PEO) - HARDER BUT POSSIBLE

**Challenges:**
- 1 year Canadian experience **required**
- More technical exams assigned
- Stricter academic assessment
- "Prejudiced toward foreigners" (forum comments)

**Tudor's Advantage:**
- **ERC Interview option** - 5+ years experience can demonstrate knowledge
- ~2/3 of international engineers qualify without technical exams via ERC

**Process:**
1. WES Course-by-Course
2. Academic assessment (may assign 1-10 exams)
3. Apply for ERC interview (13+ years experience)
4. 1 year Canadian experience
5. NPPE

**Cost:** ~$600 CAD
**Timeline:** 12-24 months

**Links:**
- https://www.peo.on.ca/licence-applications/international-engineering-graduates
- https://www.peo.on.ca/apply/become-professional-engineer/application-requirements

---

### Quebec (OIQ) - LANGUAGE BARRIER

**Challenge:** **French proficiency required by law**

**Process:**
- WES ICAP required
- Academic assessment vs Quebec programs

- French language requirement

**Not recommended** unless Tudor is fluent in French.

**Links:**

# Compliance Checklist

## Purpose
- This checklist sets the minimum compliance framing for the first buyer outreach pack.
- It does not certify compliance by itself; it identifies what must be confirmed before external commitments are made.

## Minimum Checklist

| Item | Status | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| Seller identity confirmed | In progress | Needs confirmation per included seller |
| Product labels available | In progress | Label copies should be collected for included SKUs |
| Traceability path documented | In progress | Cooperative aggregation path must be clearly stated |
| DSVSA-relevant requirements checked | In progress | Validate by product group before buyer-facing claims |
| HACCP or equivalent food-safety handling position known | In progress | Especially important where aggregation or repacking occurs |
| Shelf-life documentation available | In progress | Required for each launch SKU |
| Invoice path defined | In progress | Must identify whether invoicing runs directly or through cooperative |
| Delivery handling rules defined | In progress | Needed for wholesale commitments |

## Wave 1 Rule
- No buyer-facing pack SHALL imply full readiness for dairy or cheese unless cold-chain and handling compliance are specifically confirmed.
- The first buyer pack SHOULD present compliance as a confirmation workflow, not as a blanket claim.

## Before External Send
1. Confirm labels and traceability for included shelf-stable SKUs.
2. Confirm invoicing path and commercial entity shown to buyers.
3. Confirm that any regulatory language in the buyer brief is precise and non-overclaiming.

# Research: CHINA IMPORT BROKER (IDEA-072)
Date: 2026-04-16

## Description
Connect 2.5M Chinese factories with Romanian/EU importers via cooperative. Commission 3-5% on orders. Leverage existing company database for buyer matching.

## Market Size & Demand
- EU-China trade: EUR 1B+/day average
- China is EU's largest source of imports (EUR 500B+/year)
- Romania imports ~EUR 5-8B from China annually
- Port of Constanta: 100M tons/year, key Black Sea gateway to Central/Eastern Europe
- Thousands of Romanian SMEs import from China but lack sourcing expertise

## Fee Structure (Industry Standard 2026)
- Small orders (<$10K): 8-10% commission
- Mid-size ($10K-100K): 5-8%
- Large/repeat (>$100K): 3-5%
- Flat fee option: $100-500/order for simple orders
- Monthly retainer: $500-3,000/mo for ongoing sourcing

## Our Advantage
- 208M companies in database including Romanian importers
- Email infrastructure to reach potential buyers (2,500+/day)
- Can identify Romanian importers by NACE codes (wholesale, manufacturing)
- Constanta port proximity = natural logistics hub
- Romanian language + local trust = advantage over Chinese-based agents
- No need to be in China -- can partner with existing Chinese agents for sourcing, act as local broker/matchmaker

## Market Validated?
YES. Dozens of successful sourcing agents charge 3-10%. The model works. However, most are China-based with local presence. Romania-based China import brokers are rare -- most Romanian importers use Alibaba directly or Turkish/Italian intermediaries.

## Price Point
3-5% commission is standard for our target volume. Realistic scenarios:
- 10 orders/month x EUR 10K avg x 5% = EUR 5,000/mo
- But getting to 10 orders/month requires significant sales effort
- Alternative: flat EUR 200-500/sourcing project + 3% on fulfilled orders

## Risk
- HIGH. We have zero China sourcing expertise or factory relationships.
- No quality control capability (need partner in China).
- Currency risk, shipping delays, customs complexities.
- High trust barrier -- importers need to trust broker with large sums.
- Long sales cycle (months from first contact to first order).
- Legal liability for defective goods, late shipments.
- 20 hours setup is vastly underestimated -- this is a full business, not a side project.

## Recommendation
**SKIP** -- Despite large market, we lack the core competency (China factory relationships, QC, logistics expertise). The 3-5% commission model requires high volume to be profitable, and we'd compete with agents who are physically in China. Better to focus on what we know (recruitment, data, EU procurement). If revisited, would need a Chinese partner and at least 6 months of relationship building.

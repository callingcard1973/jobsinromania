**TITLE:** Feasibility Study – Supplying Bulk Agricultural Products via a Federation of Cooperatives (CAP) to Institutional Procurement Channels

**OBJECTIVE:**
Evaluate the feasibility of structuring **Gospodarii de Altadata Cooperativa Agricola (CAP)** as a **federation of agricultural cooperatives** to aggregate and supply bulk agricultural and food products to:

1. Romanian public procurement system (SEAP/SICAP)
2. NATO-related procurement channels in Romania (direct and contractor-based)
3. United Nations procurement systems (UNGM and affiliated agencies)

---

**SCOPE OF ANALYSIS:**

1. **Organizational Model – Federation Structure**

   * Define CAP as a federation of multiple cooperatives:

     * Membership model (criteria for joining)
     * Governance structure (central coordination vs decentralized supply)
   * Legal framework in Romania for cooperative federations
   * Contracting model:

     * CAP as prime contractor vs intermediary aggregator

2. **Supply Aggregation Capacity**

   * Mapping of member cooperatives:

     * Product categories (grains, vegetables, oils, processed goods)
     * Production volumes per cooperative
   * Consolidated supply capacity:

     * Monthly and annual tonnage
     * Ability to fulfill large contracts
   * Mechanisms for ensuring:

     * Consistent quality
     * Delivery reliability
     * Standardized specifications

3. **Procurement Channel Analysis**

   * SEAP/SICAP:

     * Eligibility of federations for public procurement
     * Relevant CPV codes for bulk agricultural supply
     * Historical contracts and key suppliers
   * NATO procurement:

     * Contractor ecosystem in Romania
     * Requirements for large-scale suppliers
   * UN procurement (UNGM):

     * Supplier registration as federation entity
     * Relevant agencies (WFP, UNICEF, UNDP)
     * Demand for bulk commodities

4. **Compliance & Standardization**

   * Harmonization of standards across member cooperatives:

     * HACCP, ISO 22000
     * EU food safety regulations
   * Centralized quality control system
   * Traceability across multiple suppliers
   * Documentation and audit readiness

5. **Logistics & Infrastructure**

   * Centralized vs regional aggregation hubs
   * Storage requirements (dry, refrigerated, frozen)
   * Transport coordination:

     * Internal collection from cooperatives
     * External distribution to clients
   * Packaging standards for institutional buyers

6. **Commercial & Contracting Strategy**

   * Direct bidding via CAP as a single entity
   * Subcontracting / supplying larger contractors
   * Framework agreements and long-term supply contracts
   * Pricing strategy for bulk volumes

7. **Financial Model**

   * Revenue aggregation model (commission vs margin-based)
   * Cost structure:

     * Logistics
     * Coordination
     * Compliance and certification
   * Working capital requirements
   * Payment distribution to member cooperatives

8. **Risk Analysis**

   * Supply inconsistency across cooperatives
   * Governance risks within federation structure
   * Price volatility in agricultural markets
   * Contract execution risks at scale
   * Dependency on institutional buyers

---

**EXPECTED OUTPUT:**

* Structured feasibility report
* Evaluation of CAP viability as a federation model
* Identification of most realistic procurement entry points
* Operational model for aggregation and delivery
* Timeline to first institutional contract

---

**CONSTRAINTS:**

* Focus strictly on bulk agricultural and food products
* Use Romania and EU regulatory context
* Avoid assumptions of immediate large-scale contracts
* Emphasize execution feasibility over theoretical structure

---

**TONE & STYLE:**

* Technical, structured, and analytical
* No marketing language

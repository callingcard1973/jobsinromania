## WFP - World Food Programme (Primary Target)

### Romania-Based Procurement
- Regional procurement hub for Eastern Europe
- CPV codes: 15200000-2, 15100000-3, 03100000-3
- Average contract value: $50,000 - $500,000 USD
- Frequency: Monthly tenders per region

### WFP Supplier Registration Requirements

1. **Basic Registration (UNGM Basic)**
   - Company profile
   - Legal documentation
   - Financial statements (2 years)
   - Product catalog
   - References

2. **Advanced Registration (WFP-specific)**
   - Food safety certificates
   - HACCP/ISO 22000
   - Traceability system
   - Production capacity verification
   - Quality control procedures

3. **Federation Eligibility**
   - Must register as single entity
   - Central contracting authority
   - Supply coordination documented
   - Quality assurance across members

## Registration Process

### Step 1: UNGM Basic Registration
- Time: 1-2 weeks
- Cost: Free
- Requirements:
  * Valid business license
  * Tax ID
  * Company description
  * Product categories

### Step 2: Level 2 Registration (for bidding)
- Time: 2-4 weeks
- Cost: Free
- Additional requirements:
  * Up-to-date product catalog
  * Reference checks
  * Compliance declaration

### Step 3: Vendor Classification
- Time: 1-2 weeks
- Result: Bronze/Silver/Gold status
- Influences contract eligibility

## Competitive Advantages for Federation

1. **Volume Commitment**
   - Can guarantee larger volumes than single cooperatives
   - WFP prefers large, reliable suppliers

2. **Geographic Coverage**
   - Multiple production zones = stable supply
   - Resilience to regional crop failures

3. **Quality Standardization**
   - Unified HACCP across members
   - Centralized QC system

4. **Price Competitiveness**
   - Economies of scale in aggregation
   - Lower average cost due to volume

## Challenges

### Federation-Specific Issues

1. **Registration Entity**
   - Must register as CAP as legal entity
   - Individual cooperatives cannot bid collectively
   - Need contractual framework between CAP and members

2. **Liability**
   - CAP assumes full delivery responsibility
   - Individual co-op failures = CAP liability
   - Need performance guarantees from members

3. **Payment Distribution**
   - UN pays CAP single entity
   - CAP must redistribute to cooperatives
   - Currency: USD (conversion risk)

4. **Traceability**
   - One invoice per contract
   - Multiple sources per invoice
   - Requires robust tracking system

## Recommended Entry Strategy

### Phase 1: Registration Preparation (Months 1-2)
- Document federation structure
- Get CAP legal registration
- Implement unified HACCP
- Build capacity database

### Phase 2: UNGM Registration (Month 3)
- Complete basic registration
- Submit WFP vendor application
- Apply for Level 2 status

### Phase 3: First Contract (Months 4-6)
- Bid on small tenders ($50,000-$100,000)
- Focus on single product (e.g., wheat)
- Build track record

### Phase 4: Scale Up (Months 6-12)
- Bid on regional tenders
- Expand product categories
- Target multi-year framework agreements

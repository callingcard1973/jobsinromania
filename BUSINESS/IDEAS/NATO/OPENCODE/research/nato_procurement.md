### Contracting Mechanism

#### Direct Supplier Routes
1. **NATO Support and Procurement Agency (NSPA)**
   - Centralized purchasing for NATO
   - Registration: https://www.nspa.nato.int/en/Suppliers/
   - Categories: Food, Food Services, Catering

2. **NATO Maintenance and Supply Agency (NAMSA)**

3. **National contractors (Romanian companies)**
   - Romanian companies winning NATO contracts
   - May subcontract food/medical supplies
   - Source: Romanian SEAP/SICAP with NATO flag

#### Registration Requirements (NSPA)
- Company registration in NATO country
- Financial stability (3 years audited statements)
- Quality certifications: ISO 9001, ISO 22000, HACCP
- Food safety compliance (EU regulations)
- Supply chain traceability system

### Estimated Demand

**NFIU-RO troops:** ~600-1,000 personnel (rotational)
**Food requirements:**
- Rations: 2,000-3,000 meals/day
- Bulk food: 500-750 kg/day
- Annual demand: 182-275 tons

**Kogălniceanu Base:**
- US/NATO personnel: 2,000-3,000
- Total food demand: 5,000-9,000 meals/day
- Bulk food: 1.5-2.5 tons/day
- Annual: 550-900 tons

### Contract Entry Points

1. **NSPA Registration**
   - Become NSPA-registered supplier
   - Bid on NSPA food tenders
   - Lead time: 6-12 months for approval

2. **Romanian Contractor Subcontracting**
   - Identify Romanian companies with NATO contracts
   - Offer bulk supply as subcontractor
   - Faster entry: 1-3 months

3. **Base-Level Procurement**
   - Local supply contracts for specific bases
   - Smaller volumes but easier access
   - Timeline: Immediate to 3 months

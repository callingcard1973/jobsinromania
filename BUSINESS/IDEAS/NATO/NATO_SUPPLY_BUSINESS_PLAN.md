**Objective:** Supply food and goods to NATO military bases in Romania and neighboring countries.

## 1. TARGET BASES IN ROMANIA

### Primary Target: Mihail Kogalniceanu (MK) Air Base
- **Location:** Constanta County, Black Sea coast
- **Status:** Expanding to become NATO's LARGEST European base
- **Investment:** $2.7 billion expansion
- **Personnel:** Currently 1,400+ US soldiers, expanding to 10,000+ permanent
- **Facilities:** DFAC (Dining Facility), barracks, MWR facilities
- **Current setup:** Local Romanian nationals provide food preparation, cleaning, supplies
- **Contracting unit:** 928th Contingency Contracting Battalion, 409th CSB

**Key insight:** "We contract for whatever services and supplies needed to support the mission. We interface with the military service, the local population and the vendor base" - Maj. Kevin Shilley, MK base

### Secondary: Deveselu Military Base
- **Location:** Olt County
- **Personnel:** 500 Romanian soldiers, 250 US troops
- **Purpose:** Aegis Ashore missile defense

### Tertiary: Campia Turzii Air Base
- **Location:** Cluj County
- **Assets:** MQ-9 Reaper drones, 571st Fighter Squadron forming

## 3. PROCUREMENT PATHWAYS

### Path A: NSPA (NATO Support and Procurement Agency) - BEST FOR FOOD
**Best for:** Larger contracts (160,000+ EUR), NATO-wide opportunities

**CRITICAL:** NSPA handles catering/food. NCIA (Communications Agency) does NOT.

**Steps:**
1. Register at https://eportal.nspa.nato.int/
2. Get listed in NSPA Source File
3. Monitor Future Business Opportunities (FBOs)
4. Respond to Requests for Proposals (RFPs)

**NSPA services include:** Catering, accommodation, camp services, maintenance

### OLSP Global Food Services (Key Framework)
NSPA manages **Outline Agreements** for:
- Food supply
- Catering staff
- Full-scale catering operations
- Rapid deployable standby for VJTF requirements

**Access:** All NATO nations and partner nations can use these services.

**Strategy:** Become a sub-contractor to existing OLSP agreement holders, or bid when new agreements are tendered.

### Path B: US Military Direct (SAM.gov)
**Best for:** US-specific contracts at MK base

**Requirements for Romanian company:**
1. Obtain NCAGE code (NATO Commercial and Government Entity)
   - Required BEFORE SAM.gov registration
   - Get from NATO Allied Committee 135 (AC/135)
2. Register at SAM.gov (free, allow 12-15 business days)
3. Obtain Unique Entity ID (UEI) - assigned during registration
4. **CRITICAL:** Need US bank account for payments
5. Annual renewal required

**Options for US banking:**
- Establish US subsidiary
- Partner with US company
- Use correspondent banking

### Path C: Local Contracting (Fastest path)
**Best for:** Immediate entry, smaller contracts

**Contact:** 928th Contingency Contracting Battalion at MK base
- They contract directly with local Romanian vendors
- Services: food preparation, supplies, equipment

**How to approach:**
1. Visit ASA Black Sea visitor center: https://home.army.mil/blacksea/about/visitors
2. Request meeting with contracting officer
3. Present capabilities and certifications

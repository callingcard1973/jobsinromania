# Research: COOPERATIVA (IDEA-011)
Date: 2026-04-16

## Description
Gospodarii de Altadata agricultural cooperative (CUI 51957925, 140 members) - aggregate small producers in Ilfov for EU export of vegetables and fruits.

## Market Size
- Romania has 23% of EU agricultural workforce but mostly fragmented smallholders (avg 0.4ha orchards)
- EU fruit/vegetable sector: Romania has most farms with orchards (23.9% of EU total) but smallest average size
- Romanian agri-food exports growing: largest EU grain exporter in 2024-2025 marketing year
- Cooperative model is proven: Tara Mea cooperative (1,000+ members) already exports to Europe, US, Persian Gulf

## Competitors
| Competitor | What | Scale | Weakness |
|-----------|------|-------|----------|
| Tara Mea Cooperative | Largest RO cooperative, Olt county | 1,000+ members, Kaufland partner | Different region, already established |
| Romalimenta | Food industry association | Trade body | Not a cooperative, no direct trade |
| Individual aggregators | Local middlemen buying from farmers | Varies | No brand, no export capability |
| Kaufland "Din Romania" | Retailer own-label sourcing | National | Buyer, not competitor (potential client) |
| Lidl "Romaneste" | Retailer own-label | National | Same - potential client |
| Polish/Bulgarian cooperatives | EU fruit/veg exporters | Large | Established supply chains |

## Our Advantage
- Legal entity already registered (CUI 51957925, June 2025)
- 140 members committed (50%+ production obligation)
- Ilfov county = near Bucharest logistics hub
- 680 RNPM mountain product producers contactable (30% response rate proven)
- AGRIP EU grant eligible (EUR 205M budget, deadline 23 Apr 2026)
- Delaware LLC structure planned for Canada/US market access via CETA

## Validated?
Yes. Tara Mea cooperative proves the model works in Romania. Kaufland partnership shows retail demand exists. Jan 2024 campaign to 666 producers got 201+ replies (30%+ rate). EU funding (AGRIP) available.

## Price
Not a subscription product. Revenue from:
- Wholesale margins (15-30% on aggregated produce)
- EU export premiums (mountain products, organic)
- AGRIP grant funding (up to 80% co-financing)

## Risk
- HIGH complexity: cooperative governance, member compliance, logistics, cold chain
- AGRIP deadline pressure (23 Apr 2026)
- Small average farm size limits volume per member
- Cash flow gap between harvest and payment
- Competition from established Eastern EU cooperatives

## Recommendation
**GO but focus** - The cooperative is already registered, members committed. Priority: submit AGRIP application before 23 Apr deadline. Parallel: sign Kaufland/Lidl supply agreements. Do NOT try to export before domestic distribution is proven. Tara Mea took 2 years before export.

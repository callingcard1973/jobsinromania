# Research: BULGARIA (IDEA-006)
Date: 2026-04-16

## Description
Email campaign to 79K Bulgarian company contacts offering construction worker placement services. Bulgaria has severe labor shortages; we supply workers from third countries (Nepal, Bangladesh, India) via Romanian recruitment infrastructure.

## Market Size & Demand
- Bulgaria labor deficit: 262,000 workers needed (2025 NEA study)
- Construction shortage: ~23,000 workers (up from 8,300 previous year — 2.8x increase)
- Construction technicians demand surged from 1,200 to 5,100 positions
- Construction sector: 21% of total labor demand (second largest after industry at 36%)
- Bulgaria needs 50,000 foreign workers for tourism alone (summer 2025)
- Bulgaria easing foreign worker hiring regulations to address structural shortage
- Average age of blue-collar workforce rising — problem is demographic and permanent
- Placement fee per worker: EUR 3,000-5,000

## Our Advantage
- **79K contacts already in PostgreSQL** — massive database of Bulgarian companies (TED + business registry)
- **Email infrastructure**: 6,300/day capacity, can reach all 79K in ~13 days
- **Existing worker pipeline**: Already placing workers in Romania (construction, factory, HORECA)
- **Romanian recruitment license**: Legal framework already in place for cross-border placement
- **Multilingual**: Can communicate in Bulgarian (similar to Romanian), English, and worker languages
- **Price advantage**: Romanian agencies often cheaper than Western European competitors
- **Domain credibility**: buildjobs.eu, factoryjobs.eu, careworkers.eu

## Market Validated? YES
- Bulgaria's 262,000 worker deficit is government-confirmed (NEA data)
- Construction shortage nearly tripled in one year (8,300 to 23,000)
- Multiple competitors already operating in this space proves paying demand
- Bulgaria actively changing legislation to ease foreign worker hiring
- EU freedom of movement allows Romanian agencies to operate in Bulgaria
- Tourism sector alone needs 50,000 workers — construction adds 23,000 more

## Price Point
- Placement fee: EUR 3,000-5,000 per construction worker placed
- Monthly temporary staffing margin: EUR 200-500/worker/month
- Target: 50 placements in year 1 = EUR 150K-250K revenue
- Scale: 200 placements/year = EUR 600K-1M
- Can also charge Bulgarian employers for database access / worker CVs

## Risk
- **Language barrier** — campaign needs Bulgarian translation (or English for larger companies)
- **Cross-border legal complexity** — Bulgarian labor regulations, posting of workers directive
- **Payment collection** — invoicing Bulgarian companies from Romania
- **Competition from local BG agencies** — they have local relationships
- **Worker supply** — need to actually have workers ready to deploy to Bulgaria
- **79K contacts quality** — need email verification, many may bounce
- **Romania also has shortages** — placing workers in Bulgaria means fewer for Romanian clients

## Recommendation: LAUNCH
Massive validated demand (262K deficit, 23K in construction alone), 79K contacts ready, zero data acquisition cost. Bulgaria is geographically adjacent and culturally similar. Even at 0.1% conversion from 79K emails = 79 interested companies = potential for 100+ placements at EUR 3-5K each. Campaign cost is effectively zero (only email sending time).

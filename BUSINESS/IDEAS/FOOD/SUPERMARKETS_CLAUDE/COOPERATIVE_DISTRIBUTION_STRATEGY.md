# Cooperative Food Distribution Strategy — Romania

## Overview

Strategy for a chamber of commerce of cooperatives distributing fresh produce, dairy, meat, and processed food across Romania. The cooperative handles the full range of food products and holds DSVSA veterinary-sanitary authorizations.

## Regulatory Requirements

### DSVSA Registration (Mandatory)

Directia Sanitara Veterinara si pentru Siguranta Alimentelor — required for ALL food handling.

- **42 county DSVSA offices** — one per county
- **Registration types**:
  - Inregistrare sanitara veterinara (basic, for storage/transport)
  - Autorizatie sanitara veterinara (full, for processing/production)
- **Timeline**: 30 days for registration, 60 days for authorization
- **Cost**: Administrative fees only (taxa timbru)
- **Contact**: dsvsa.ro — county office list with addresses, phones, emails

### ANSVSA Certifications

Autoritatea Nationala Sanitara Veterinara si pentru Siguranta Alimentelor.

- National oversight body above county DSVSA offices
- Issues export certificates for EU trade
- Maintains approved establishment list (NRL)
- Contact: ansvsa.ro

### HACCP (Mandatory)

Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points — required by EU law.

- Mandatory for all food businesses (EU Reg. 852/2004)
- Self-implemented or via certified consultant
- HACCP plan must cover: receiving, storage, processing, transport, delivery
- Annual audit and documentation review

### ISO 22000 (Recommended)

Food Safety Management System — valued by major chains.

- International standard, recognized by all supermarket chains
- Combines HACCP with management system
- Certification by accredited bodies (RENAR in Romania)
- Major chains (Kaufland, Lidl, Carrefour) prefer ISO 22000 certified suppliers

### Cooperative Legal Framework

- **Legea 1/2005** — privind organizarea si functionarea cooperatiei
- **Cooperative types**: Grade I (production), Grade II (county union), Grade III (national federation)
- **Tax advantages**: Cooperatives can benefit from reduced taxation
- **MADR subsidies**: Ministry of Agriculture programs for cooperatives
- **AFIR funding**: EU rural development funds (PNDR 2021-2027)

### Traceability

- **EU Reg. 178/2002** — General Food Law, mandatory traceability
- **Lot tracking** — from farm to fork, one step back / one step forward
- **Recall capability** — must be able to recall products within 24h
- **Digital traceability** — increasingly required by major chains

## Key Institutional Contacts

### DSVSA County Offices (42 counties)

Required for sanitary-veterinary registration. Full list at dsvsa.ro.

### MADR — Ministry of Agriculture

- Subsidies for agricultural cooperatives
- Market intervention programs
- Export support programs
- Contact: madr.ro

### Chambers of Commerce (CCIR Network)

- 42 county chambers of commerce
- Business registration, market information
- Trade missions and matchmaking
- CCIR central: ccir.ro

### ANPC — Consumer Protection

- Compliance requirements for food labeling
- Romanian language labeling mandatory
- Nutritional information (EU Reg. 1169/2011)
- Contact: anpc.ro

### AFIR — Rural Development Agency

- EU funding for cooperative investments (PNDR)
- Sub-measure 4.2: Investments in processing agricultural products
- Sub-measure 16.4: Short supply chains and local markets
- Contact: afir.ro

### APIA — Payments and Intervention Agency

- Direct agricultural payments
- Market support programs
- Contact: apia.org.ro

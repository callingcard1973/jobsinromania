# First Outreach Plan

## Objective
- Turn the current FOOD assets into a first commercial wave that can produce buyer conversations quickly.
- Use existing supply, cooperative structure, and buyer data instead of waiting for a full platform build.

## Core Offer
- Commercial identity: cooperative-led Romanian mountain and regional food aggregation.
- First product focus: honey, preserves, juices, vinegar, and a limited cheese line.
- Sales angle: local sourcing, shelf-stable assortment first, premium Romanian origin, and later cold-chain expansion.

## Product Pack For First Wave
- Honey: standard jars and gift-ready variants.
- Preserves: fig jam and related shelf-stable fruit products.
- Juice and vinegar: natural juice line and apple vinegar.
- Optional premium line: selected cheese and dairy only where cold-chain handling is confirmed.

## Campaign Structure

### Campaign 1: wholesale-first
- Targets: Metro Romania, Selgros Romania, La Doi Pasi via Metro, regional wholesale markets.
- Message: easy onboarding, mixed producer assortment, shelf-stable first, cooperative invoice flow.
- Goal: first meeting, assortment review, packaging requirements, delivery constraints.

### Campaign 2: retail-chain local sourcing
- Targets: Kaufland Romania, Carrefour Romania, Mega Image, Profi.
- Message: Romanian local sourcing, curated mountain-product line, pilot regional assortment.
- Goal: identify listing path, buyer requirements, and paperwork gaps.

### Campaign 3: network and expansion routes
- Targets: diaspora shops, Bucharest restaurants, Bucharest hotels, online Romanian-product stores.
- Message: differentiated Romanian origin products, low-friction shelf-stable packs, seasonal curated bundles.
- Goal: close smaller first orders and gather proof of sell-through.

## Outreach Pack To Prepare Before Sending
1. One-page cooperative presentation.
2. First 10-seller sheet with categories and readiness.
3. First 15-20 SKU sheet with pack sizes and indicative pricing.
4. Delivery coverage and lead-time note.
5. Compliance checklist: DSVSA, HACCP, traceability, label readiness.
6. Clear contact point for buyer follow-up.

## 7-Day Execution Sequence
1. Confirm the first 10 sellers for stock, packaging, shelf life, and invoice readiness.
2. Build three buyer lists: wholesale, retail, and alternative channels.
3. Prepare one outreach email and one short buyer deck.
4. Send Wave 1 to wholesale targets first.
5. Use responses to refine pricing, packs, and paperwork.
6. Send Wave 2 to retail buyers once the offer is tightened.
7. Use Wave 3 to generate faster pilot orders while larger buyer conversations mature.

## Success Criteria
- 5 buyer replies.
- 2 live meetings or calls.
- 1 pilot order or assortment review.
- 1 validated product shortlist for broader rollout.
